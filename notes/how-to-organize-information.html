I spent a lot of time during the weekend reading something I had put off for some time. It were Tiago Forte's posts on note taking and organizing information. Before I flood with links, a quick context. Evernote used to be one of my favorite note taking apps but without much organization, it became quite not manageable. Also, once Evernote took away the ability to create notes from email forwards from the free plan, I lost interest because I suspected the sustainability of the company to rely too much on it. Have seen this movie before when free products start removing features instead of growing and adding more features for paid tiers. Recently I tend to keep the tabs open in browser to be looked at again and atleast 3 times in last 12 months has the browser crashed and I have lost all tabs. I also tend to send to Pocket, however , once again without a good organization and To-Do or Review tied to Pocket, it's a big storage that I probably never go back to other than for searching read articles for a re-read. So the pain points I was trying to address were
  • Organize information so that it is actionable
  • Reasonable sort so I know to find them easily ( tags got out of hand in both Evernote and Pocket)
Tiago's Building a Second Brian philosophy (and course) seems to address that. Before the course he posted the framework in posts, which is what I binged during the weekend. In addition I had come across his post on Taking Smart Notes ( Zettlekasten method)  earlier this year, as I had subscribed to his newsletter. That seemed very interesting, and for some reason I thought it was same as his method P.A.R.A. I think there are differences, and I am inclined to try some aspects of it. At least I closed all the browser tabs, and decided to pocket the articles I come across during the day and read from pocket if needed. I also set up a Pocket -> Trello, and going to use Trello for P.A.R.A method and see how it works out. I also have to practice adding a note while I save an article. I use office One Note on phone, otherwise would have tried with One Note. But Trello might work better because I've tried to use it for ToDo in the past.
If I need to summarize one difference it would be this - I think I'll have more chances in P.A.R.A method to actually go back to the said article and use it, instead of just throwing it in Pocket and will never have time to go back through them.
