Why technical experts make great leaders: Podcast

September 03, 2018
overcast.fm/+DihwJf0
Goodall is the co-author of the HBR articles “If Your Boss Could Do Your Job, You’re More Likely to Be Happy at Work” and “Why the Best Hospitals Are Managed by Doctors.”
I had mixed feelings about this episode - or mostly disagreements. I have had leaders who were generalists and technical experts ; good ones and terrible ones - in all combinations and I'm just one data point. So I don't quite agree that a technical expert makes a great leader. The podcast author/speaker does caution that well trained (for management) technical leaders are better and not anyone strong technically, but that is theoritcal and hypothetical. Some of the leadership qualities can't be trained. There was only one point I agreed with - it seems the employees who know their boss can cover them in their absence are happier. That is bit obvious. In addition according to me, a manager who knows exactly the job of people she is managing can relate or understand the efforts better. But knowing doesn't mean being able to do it, so it doesn't require technical expertise, only the ability to listen and learn - which are key qualities of a leader anyways.
