My experience with 911

October 06, 2020

Just after a road trip to Maine and other places for 3 days, I was to return to work on Monday. I had half mind several times to apply for paid time off on Monday to take rest, however life had other plans.

I woke up from sleep because of stomach pain at 4am. And went going to toilet for couple of times, hoping that would make the pain go away. When it didn't subside, and when I realized it was a different pain, and there was uneasyness in my stomach, I wondered if it was due to some food poisoning. I had eaten all sorts of food the previous day, including half a burger (which I was so angry against), nachos, croissant (which wife was so angry against and threw away the remaining one), and breakfast burger - it was hard to say which one had caused it the food poisoning. I thought it was either food poisoning or indigestion - I leaned more towards food poisoning as the pain was different. I decided to drink some water which made my stomach uneasy and me nauseous. I decided to forcibly puke - it made me temporarily feel better and I repeated it for 7-8 or more times.

The pain continued until 8 when wife woke up.

I couldn't tolerate anymore and thought of options - asked wife to call Sandy, as he knows about medicines of which painkiller might be good. At this time I was getting weaker and pain was constant. I thought of using 911 as that was possibly quickest option and they may take care even along the way or before going to hospital I thought. Also vaguely remembered Americans calling 911 to complain people sitting in park and this was more important use of 911. And I pay taxes and have a healthcare, why not.

I remember my classmate had used 911 to get her kidney stone removed. I knew kidney stone pain is sharp and intolerable. For me after 6 hours this was beyond intolerable.

They came and started asking questions - first question was how was I doing ?! And subsequently they kept talking to me even along the way which I remember reading somewhere they do to keep you awake and conscious. Asking ssn, and something else - which was really taxing to recall and answer. But I realized I wasn’t treated like an emergency and the lights and siren weren’t on and he stopped along the way in traffic - so I realized it was just a quick Uber for me. :) Have to see where it gets billed and who foots the bill.

At least I hoped I would get quick treatment after I went there - which was a big disappointment. When I went to ER myself few years ago, was impressed with all the quick attention - at least initially with a quick pain killer. Here they took a long time - from parking the vehicle to going inside and then to get vitals, to get a nurse (who took like an hour or more) and I kept moaning in pain and pleading for help. I literally begged for someone to do something for the pain and all a lady did was to close the door. It was the most inhumane feeling. I couldn’t even cry as I didn’t have energy. Crying needs energy.

Nurse finally came and a doctor came I think, who ordered pain killer which nurse has to order someone else to administer. Such an inefficient system. Even the painkiller took time to take effect. Then I closed my eyes for a bit but was feeling cold and I had to ask several times - there was no blanket and temperature increase in room did nothing.

While tired and dying in pain, these nurses ask too many questions. Was very irritated. Many are repeated but two were different. One was do you want to list a religion - I said some nasty things in mind and said no, thanks in words. Second was she said I was listed single - I thought of making some joke like “oh you telling me I still got a chance then” or “glad my wife doesn’t know that” , but thought the nurse wasn’t the kind that would appreciate humor and I didn’t want to answer further questions, so just said no. But was proud of myself 🤣

I was ordered CT scan around 2, transport pulled me near ct scan room which was literally two rooms away and it was too cold in the hallway. Finally someone handed me a blanket. But it didn’t help much. Around 3.30 ct scan completed I think.

Another doctor visited at 4 and said there is high wbc and they suspect appendicitis and will have to consult her team. I said there is too much pain and she said she will get back soon. For more than an hour there was no sign. Pain has become like mornings again as the pain killer wore off by this time. That’s when I cried out.

I kept going out - which I didn’t know I was not supposed to due to covid and if I used the remote they would just ignore it either for blanket or for the pain. Nurse said he can’t give pain killer until they decide on appendectomy but they are held up in another case and he was checking with them. He didn’t check with them until I went back again after 20 min. And once they did suggest he could order another pain killer, he still didn’t order it immediately until I went back and stared at him, as if I was lost.

Finally it was 6 pm another assistant administered painkiller. To all these folks I asked if they had a spare iPhone charger and none did. The doctor who had gone to check with her team did and she said she would lend but if she was so hurried and forgotten about my pain, high chance she would remember charger. I’m thinking of making a first aid type hospital kit with charger, pen, book and other necessities.

Around 7, another big doctor explained about appendix and how it’s an useless organ and only chickens have use of it for immunity. it would be better to remove it now rather than wait. I said ok. I was ready to lose anything to get rid of pain let alone an useless organ.

Then an hour later , another doctor who is the actual surgeon came. An Indian. He asked the background-by this time I was expert at giving a short summary. He asked me if I was clear on the procedure. I asked if pain killer wears off before surgery - he said no it will be no time before surgery. That was at 8. Surgery started at 11.30.

They did the covid swab test and the results were also a prerequisite to surgery. This may have been part of the delay as well.

All thru out the evening I kept sleeping to something playing on tv - either Harry Potter or Biden. That was the best feeling.

The machine would beep continuously and I call for help .. no one would come .. at one point I felt smashing it dead. Pressing no buttons would stop it - after 10 min, I kept calling the nurse until someone came.

Once I went up for surgery everything was good - nurses were making jokes, helpful, even found a charger as my phone was at 1%. I was shivering and they gave two warm blankets just to get to OR! I definitely had a fever. I also had severe heartburn and acidity. And thirst. Changed into gown. Two people had met talking to me about anesthesia and their name was Catalina or something. But when the second introduced herself I thought she was testing me and I said haven’t we met before and she said no. I thought she must have been near anasthesia so much she must have lost her memory.

Around 1.30 surgery must have completed. I woke up at 2.30. they gave me crackers. Moved me into post surgery room where nurses were very helpful. The room was nicer. Bed was nicer. Plenty of blankets. Nurse would come promptly and even insist I call her. I kept falling asleep but the nurse would check on hourly or so. It was two different worlds pre and post surgery. I do like this hospital where my daughter was born and have several good impressions about it. I hope the neglect and apathy for my treatment pre surgery was just an anomaly.

Nurse said while “property” sign, that I make sure I am going with all the things I came with. I said one thing is missing. She looked at me puzzlingly. I said my appendix. Was proud of my timing and delivery. 🤣 Being tired out of my mind and just woken up. She had a nice clapback with “that’s a good thing to leave behind”

Had breakfast and another round of doctors came and explained the recovery process. And some time after I was ready to check out. Sandy came and picked and was home for lunch.
