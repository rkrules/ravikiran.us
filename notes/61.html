July 23, 2018
From Quartz daily brief
Despite bumper earnings, bank shares have lagged the overall market this year. The likely reason is that investors think the US, and perhaps the global economy, is getting closer to its next downturn. It has been nine years since the last recession, almost double the average for the US economic cycle. The bull market in US stocks is also nine years old, the second-longest run on record. Unemployment is near historic lows following an unprecedented stretch of job gains. Other signs of late-cycle activity, like risky US subprime lending (paywall), are popping up. And then there’s escalating trade war between the US and the rest of the world, or the fact the Federal Reserve is raising rates while other central banks keep them at historic lows.
