OPOS is a set of techniques with one goal - to empower anyone to cook anything perfectly, in minutes. Even recipes you have never seen or heard before. How is this possible? What can replace manual skill & decades of experience? Standard recipes and equipment. OPOS standardises everything so that even a child can cook as well as an experienced chef, as long as standard equipment is used and standard recipes are followed. Imagine you are drawing a free hand circle. That's traditional cooking. It takes a huge amount of effort and time to learn. Even then, consistency is not possible. In OPOS we replace this with a compass. Suddenly anyone can draw a circle perfectly, everytime. OPOS is built on a deskilled cooking technique called Pressure Baking. It is about cooking food at high heat, for a very short time, without water, in its own juices. All OPOS recipes aim to do this. This fills food with life. You see real magic. Colours, textures, flavours and tastes you have not experienced before. It will freak you out, the first time. Cooking at high heat, for a short time, without water is the best way to maximise colour, texture, taste, nutrition and flavour. You see that in stir frying, You see that in Tandoori cooking, barbecuing and grilling. But these cooking styles demand years of experience. OPOS deskills it with the right equipment - the MagicPot. This is nothing but an ordinary pressure cooker that has been redesigned to work on high heat, without water. Yes, you can use a normal pressure cooker to start with, but remember the manufacturers warn you against using normal cookers on high heat or without water. It is risky, especially with advanced OPOS recipes. OPOS takes 10 minutes to learn. These ten minutes of learning can feed you good food for a lifetime. All you have to do is Lesson 1 & Lesson 2. Lesson 1: Standardisation Standardise your cooker and heat source. Add 1/4C water (60ml) in your 2L cooker. Close and fix weight. Cook on 'high'. Check if you get a long and loud whistle in 1 to two minutes. (On gas, High heat means the flame just about covers the base of your cooker. On Induction, High is 900 to 1200W depending on the model). Electric stoves need preheating. Tips: 1.Whistles earlier than 1 minute? Decrease heat. 2. Whistles after 2 minutes? Increase heat. 3. Handles getting hot? Change burner/ use induction Lesson 2: Pressure Baking In your 2L Cooker, add 1/4C water. Add 2C chopped beans, 1/2tsp salt, 1 chopped green chilli. Close, place weight and cook on high for 2 whistles/ 4 minutes. Release pressure. Mix all. Marvel at the colour. 1. Excess water? Reduce water the next time. 2. Undercooked? Go for 3 or 4 whistles. These simple techniques let you make make magic in your kitchen. You can cook anything, from any cuisine, much better than experienced chefs, in minutes.

All good? Let's start ! Lesson 1: Heat 1/4C (60ml) water in your 2L cooker on high so that you get the first whistle between 1 and 2 minutes. If your timing is less than 1 minute, decrease heat. If it is over 2 minutes, increase heat. (Electric/ ceramic/ glass top stoves are sluggish. They need to be preheated for 5 to 20 minutes before you place the cooker). Unable to get a timing of under 2 minutes? Change your burner/buy an induction stove. Handles heating up? Getting a burning smell? The flames are coming up the sides of the cooker. Use a different burner/buy an induction. Lesson 2: Pressure Baking In a 2L cooker, add 1tsp oil, 1/4C water, 2C finely chopped beans, 1/4tsp salt, 1/4tsp chilli powder. Cook on high for 2 whistles. Release pressure. Mix all. Like it? Move to lesson 3. Undercooked? Do not release pressure. Overcooked? Cook for just one whistle. Water remaining?: Reduce water next time. Lesson 3: More Pressure Baking Repeat lesson 2 with other vegetables. Use fresh/ frozen. Reduce/ eliminate water. Reduce/ eliminate oil. Vary cut size. Use a mix of veggies. Vary quantity. Do not Pressure bake beyond 5 minutes. If pressure does not build up in the first 3 minutes, switch off, add more water and repeat. Get comfortable with this core technique. Experiment ! Lesson 4: Pressure Baked meals : Tawa Pulav Mix flash cooked vegetables with a starch to make a meal. https://youtu.be/o2UEA3xlwD8 Lesson 5: Pressure Baked soups : Tomato soup Flash & blend spiced vegetables into soups. https://youtu.be/rlS1DWrv3wY Leson 6: Pressurebaked curries: Kurma Layer & flash with a curry paste. Blend 1/2C chopped coconut, 6 cashews, 1tsp each (salt, OPOS ginger-garlic paste, garam masala),1/8tsp turmeric powder, 1 green chilli to a thick paste. In a 2L cooker, layer as below: Layer1: 1/4C water, 1tbsp ghee Layer 2: 1/2C each chopped (carrot, beans,potato, capsicum) Layer 3: Coconut paste (see above) Layer 4: Close & fix weight. Cook on high for 2 whistles/ 6minutes. Let settle. Mix in 1/2C coconut milk/ milk. Serve with roti/ idiappam/ appam. Don't have fresh coconut? Use cashew/ almond paste instead. Lesson 7: Extending Pressurebaking to create your own variations: Repeat lesson 6 by adding/ removing layers as you like: Layer 1: 1/4C water/ stock, 2tsp fat (your fav Oil/ Ghee/ Butter) Layer 2: 2C chopped vegetables/ mix Layer 3: 1/2C coconut paste (or cashew/ almond/peanut paste) Layer 4: 1tbsp poppy seed paste (or sesame/ pumpkin/ other seeds...) Layer 5: 2tsp green chilli paste (Or red chillies/ paprika/ pepper..) Layer 6: 1/2tsp Ginger- Garlic paste, 1/2 tsp salt . Layer 7: 1/4tsp turmeric (or saffron) Layer 8: 1tbsp Caramelised onions Layer 9: 1tbsp Caramelised tomato Layer 10: 1tbsp Mint (Or basil/ curry leaves/ parsley/ rosemary/....)
