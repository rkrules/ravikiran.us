Some kids lose teeth quietly. You are not one of those kids.

Every tooth has been an event. A negotiation. A letter-writing opportunity. A chance to quiz someone (real or magical) or rope them into your ongoing creative projects. This is the running record.

## Tooth #1 — September 15, 2024

Your first tooth came out after a lot of wiggling, in India. You were very curious about the whole lifecycle — where do teeth go, why do they fall out, what happens next. The questions started here and never really stopped.

![First tooth, lots of excitement](/uploads/tooth-fairy/one.jpg)

![Still in India](/uploads/tooth-fairy/three.jpg)

## Tooth #2 — October 17, 2024

This one wiggled for even longer before it finally gave up. You were patient. Patience is not always your thing, but for teeth you'll wait it out.

## Tooth #3 — November 26, 2024

You left a note for the tooth fairy. Asked her name. Left her a little present. And then — because why do things halfway — you also recruited her to help continue a story you were writing. Outsourcing your creative work to magical beings. Respect.

![The note and the present](/uploads/tooth-fairy/four.jpg)

![The story collaboration request](/uploads/tooth-fairy/five.jpg)

## Tooth #6 — October 26, 2024

This time you quizzed the tooth fairy. An actual quiz. The night before, you briefly suspected your dad had stolen the presents Santa gave you — then, in a split second, pivoted to asking if your dad *was* Santa. Full investigation mode.

The magic is still intact. Barely.
