Roadmap and customer meeting

November 11, 2014
Business

Over the last few days, I attended two meetings of two hours each. One was a roadmap planning with the PMs, and the other was meeting a customer/partner - more of a distributor of our brand and apps but we both get revenue advantage.

In the roadmap meeting, it was almost 1 hour 50 minute when the first concern/mention of customer value. Well I could have brought it up, but I wanted to play along. All the roadmap items except the one which actually brought in the “customer” mention, were actually already decided by “management” team or an another internal team. To be fair, in all possibility they might have already figured out ‘customer value’. Also, to be fair, many of these items are more about growth ( expansion of countries or localization). 

But there are problems here - all employees, no matter who, need to be thinking about end consumer. If some other team has already evaluated, it needs to be known to all employees - at least the ones who are implementing. This is about transparency, communication, clarity and about empowerment. 

All meetings should talk about end user, no matter how obvious it is. At times it is going to be uncomfortable - but there is no harm in emphasizing the users/customers. It is better than losing the focus.

And finally, during the roadmap discussion effort needs to be brought into discussion along with value. Without this one can not accurately prioritze the features. Instead what ended up was a laundry list left to higher management to prioritize - who would prioritize based on the value or importance ( may or may not considering end user value, but definitely not using effort).

*

For the sake of simplicity, let’s say our partner is our customer and end user is their customer.

Second meeting was comparatively interesting. Sales team constantly talked about “value” to our customer (partner), who did also bring up value or end user discussion couple of times. It was much earlier (around 1 hour mark) that this happened.

It is easy to note that incentive pays a big role here - sales team is incentivized to create better deals for their clients and the company. However it is difficult to measure product team’s success without clear goals and ability to track them. And tracking a product success along various angles such as revenue, increase in competitive space, increase in brand, user experience and many others are especially difficult in a consumer application space. 

On the other hand, my other complaints did surface in this meeting (refer this post) . Most of the product / business ideas were about 'build it they will come attitude’ or based on the assumptions and lacked either consumer research or data or experimentation. The confidence boggles me, but then we were talking about gazillion billion dollar company (not that they are always right, I have often found mistakes in their marketing or products, but they have been successful nevertheless).
