How to operate

November 07, 2014
Business

I have followed many startup posts/stories and these days Tue and Thurs are my favorite days due to http://startupclass.samaltman.com/ ! Very good class today from Keith. (post updated after a day after annotations were available)

Many things resonated with me, few highlights:

1. Hire/ promote specialists to managers.  People management can be learned later but the skills can’t be. If a non-expert is made to manage experts just because he is good with people management, it is very demotivating. 

2. Focus on A+ things even though they are hard instead of B+ which may be slightly easier. Together with singular focus, which is important in smaller companies.

3. Culture is the framework through which decisions are made.

4. Calendar management, weekly meetings where employee sets agenda, weekly once or biweekly. Interestingly my meeting with my manager used to be like this. 

5. Thoughts on “editing”, “writing” and delegating. When to delegate and when to micromanage.

6. His thoughts about open-ness/transparency. I know that Buffer has pretty great open philosophy including compensation. 

7. Metrics, dashboard and the importance of anomalies.

Lot to learn from this talk.
