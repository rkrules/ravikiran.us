Why do people think Apple Pay is so innovative—an equivalent feature has been part of Android for more than two years? - Quora

This answer is brilliant and should be mandatory reading for MBAs and Product Managers. Product and Marketing are both equally important according to me, although I know people place different weights on them depending upon their biases. A strong product without marketing is as useless as a strong marketing without a good product. 

Nothing says that more than Apple Pay (I’m not that convinced about Apple watch, but that’s for another day) in the recent news. As many other features on Android, Google Wallet has been around for years only to surface on Apple this week. (Other features such as widgets, 3rd party keyboards - few other things that made into iOS8 recently). However it never got the mass adoption that Apple Pay has and will get. (1M activations in 72 hours).

The reason is simple - not only Apple nails it in marketing (as seen in the above link), but also nails it in the product and execution. Apple released with many credit card issuers, banks, merchants lined up on day 1; made it so that none of carriers can block Apple Pay citing their proprietary payment solutions (Verizon and others blocked Google Wallet ! Google was left to use Google Wallet only on Nexus devices).

*

I think the problem is with the culture of design/experience first approach by Apple. They look at a problem, and then attack with a solution. It doesn’t hurt that they are amongst the most valuable companies to recruit talented engineers. However, Google is primarily engineering company and they build great products - partnership, negotiations and  marketing are afterthoughts.
