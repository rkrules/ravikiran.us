On Amazon Echo

November 08, 2014
Business

Amazon introduced a new device this week - Echo. It is a voice activated bluetooth speaker. My reactions to it are mixed.

Good:

I am happy and I like it because Amazon created a new device and a new use case. I always cringed about voice activated devices, and thought in-car is the only valid use case but never thought about in-house.

The fact that Amazon of all the companies thought and came up with this box is very interesting -  for it’s uses, Google should have been the obvious one to release this but Microsoft or Yahoo, Spotify, iTunes, Google Music, Apple would have been good places for this device to come from. Or even Evernote ?

Also, one more device that is not a ‘personal’ but a shared device interests me. 

Bad:

Voice activated devices hardly make sense compared to the hype they manage to create - be it Siri, Cortona, Google Now’s voice search (which I hate that they failed to brand) or be it on Google Wear/Glass. Who talks to devices - it is such annoying thing to do, and there are only two cases when you would do it - when you are alone, or when your hands are tied. Or make it 'and’ instead of 'or’. Of course, the surroundings need to be silent for it to work. It would be interesting to see how these devices would take off ( would Google and Apple be left behind if this device becomes a success ?) (Siri was long supposed to kill Google, last I checked, Siri has been a good pastime and nothing otherwise. Thiis was also something I predicted when Siri was introduced).

Second, how does this add to Amazon ? No one will ever shop from a voice activated device on Amazon.  (Or may be once a set list of items is ready, Alexa ship me item 4). . So, other than Amazon music, the way this would help Amazon is to create shopping lists ( are reminder lists so popular on Amazon ?). Do they want to increase Prime membership ? Would the device such as this cause a dent in the membership - similar to Fire phone bundling with Prime I am not sure if the device increases Prime membership or just targeted to Prime members. I think it is latter.

Pricing: $200 is probably absurd, so let’s consider Amazon is only targeting Prime members.  $100 is decent pricing, but not Amazonian pricing. A sub 60 or 70 would have been ideal pricing.

*

Problem:

As a good product manager, I can’t not think what problem is this product solving ? Let’s look at the marketing:

“Always ready, connected and fast. Just ask” - there is nothing new here than other voice activated products such as Siri or Google Now voice search.

More details include key terms such as:

* Info, music, news, weather

* Hands free 

Two other bullets about getting smarter, voice recognition across room aren’t key because they are necessary features if this device has to get any traction.

From the above two points, through other marketing material there are two distinct use cases:

1) To ask for information (definition, information, news, weather)

2) To make Alexa do some work (note taking/reminders, play music)

#1 is usually what Google, Siri have done and done well. Traditional voice search. Search has traditionally been monetized by ads, and voice doesn’t really a good platform to monetize it.

#2 is not done by many products (there are personal assistants in handheld devices that can do this including S voice, and Siri). But this is where there is business opportunity for Amazon. A small robot in everyone’s house.  

What the marketing material is not emphasizing (and they should) is that this is a “in-home” “entertainment” “shared” device & bluetooth speaker. 

Of all the things “search” ,  "music" , “note taking” - it would be interesting to see which use case will take off. My bet is it would be 70% music, 25% search and may be 5% reminders.

Update:

After thinking more, there is a huge potential opportunity if Echo opens it’s platform to work with IoT. See this video: http://t.co/z5I96Uwuqy

And also, phone integration would be very powerful.

*

And lastly, what data or customer research went into creating this product. That may not be negative, but I am just curious similar to wondering which search engine is powering this technology behind Echo.

*

It is an intriguing device and very happy Amazon is innovating on devices where others have failed even though Amazon failed on phone.
