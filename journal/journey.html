Business

I don’t know yet if this is going to be a company/startup, right now it is just a product in prototype. But I wanted to log some of my experiences.

Beginnings

One of the professors I know has had this idea for too many years now. He intimately feels the pain of the students who find it hard to study one of his fond subjects. He is equally averse to technology and running a business.

I am an experienced product manager, always reading up on startup and product management. I have had great success and growth internal to the company, but I haven’t done something from scratch, independently. So I wanted to pick this opportunity and offered to help him/partner with him.

We knew what the problem was. Check empathy. And professor came up with how we can help to solve the problem using technology. I put my marketing and management experience behind it. 

Brainstorming

Our brainstorming for weeks included different design prototypes/wireframes we hand sketched. I used little bit of invision but it was mostly hand drawn.

Along with product brainstorms, significant business brainstorming also happened - is this a consumer product ? How to make it go viral ? Will it be a push strategy or a pull strategy ? How do we reach target market ? How do we make it sticky ?

Initially we thought it was a consumer product (students as consumers), and we would work a pull strategy. Also to work with text book distributors who may be looking for a companion digital product to their books.

After much thinking I realized the pain to market to an entirely new set of consumers every year/term. The virality would never succeed beyond among the same batch of students. Taking a step back we examined text book model - and realized the real power lies with the professors.

There is one minor problem here, the product is potentially helping students learn the subject in a different way than professors would teach, sometimes even skipping the terminologies. This might irk professors. But at the present stage, it appears that Professors hold the key to distribution.

Knowing the real user might help us in our pitch. Yet, we need to appeal to end users as well. This is very tricky and I am very excited to be figuring this out.

Again, distribution through Professors mean it is similar to B2B. We need to find places where these Profs meet and hang out. Understand what appeals to them. Tweak our solution to meet their goal and make their life easier.

If we succeed, we might end up changing how a particular subject is taught in future.

I can already see that this concept can be expanded to several other subjects and topics.

Competitive research

Our competitive research began by looking at similar offerings in the industry on Apple, Web and Android platforms. We found some competition, yet they are somewhat painful to endure. This convinced us more to help address the problem and solve a pain in the students. It is to be noted that many of these students are ‘required’ to take this subject and thus increasing the pain.

Naming

Equity Share:

There are ton of resources on how to split equity but none that applied directly to our scenario. Also I didn’t want to overthink it. At the beginning I just wanted to help my Professor, and I had been grateful to him earlier during my job search for his referrals and recommendations. He had been a great mentor. At some point he quoted a number as my share, which was different than what I was dreaming. Hence to clear the air, we decided to come up with a number. He said 'I rather want you to be very happy than happy, happy than sad". In other words he was not trying to screw me over. At the same time I didn’t want to exploit or get into a bargaining game, so I wanted some guidance or data about how it is done.

I referred many articles , and got a range from different tools. But none actually was directly applicable to our scenario. So I decided to keep it real simple : money, idea, overall management and decided to split the pie threeways. I wanted to downplay idea, as I believe execution is everything and my co-founder has repeatedly agreed that without me he would have never gone this far. But didn’t want to get into a bargaining game, so agreed to split equally. I took 1/3rd share. Next day there was a discussion of how management didn’t include distribution which is key 4th component and one in which again my partner would play a bigger role. I think nitpicking wasn’t great and said let’s just split 3 ways..if we do 4 ways, then i would be half component on the last on, making it 62.5, 37.5

Convincing naysayers: 

There were lot of naysayers to this project. And they were right. One of them said, he was a bad student never bothered to study or buy books. Of course that is a valid point - our customers are going to be a subsection of entire student market - as is in any market sizing problem. In addition, similar to old saying of every problem is an opportunity - it is a problem because textbooks are expensive and no one likes to read long text. Wouldn’t our interactive game at a reasonable price help address it ? That’s our assumption, we need to talk to users to confirm.

There were other concerns that sharing idea in a prototype would make users steal the idea. Kind of valid, however it is much expensive not to test ; and there are always better ways to implement this. I am a big believer of execution rather than idea.

Another opposition was that this is not a large market, and would drain our resources in maintaining this. It is not profitable. This is largely wrong and I just gave the benefit of doubt that the person wasn’t well aware with apps market.
