Manual of Me

November 19, 2020

Hi, I am Ravikiran. I help teams build useful technology products on the web and mobile. I believe in the power of collaboration and empathy. I have practiced that belief in my career as a software developer and as a product management leader.

Manual of Me

Based on guides such as this , this , this, and this. This document is to give a sense of what's it like to work with me. I am hoping this will be a living document, which I will revise from time to time.

  1. My style
    • I am a data-oriented Product Manager, my focus is on providing a great end user experience
    • Learning and helping are two of my personal values. Product sense, curiosity and empathy are my leadership values. 
    • Communication: I usually respond to emails within a day or two. It is very rare that I haven’t responded to an email for more than two days. In some cases, the email response will be delayed when I am waiting on someone to provide me information that I need to respond with – I usually send an acknowledgement email in such cases. I also appreciate if you can qualify your asks with a timing expectation from you. 
    • Follow ups: If you don’t hear from me for a week or in case of other time bound requests, I don’t mind the follow ups.  I follow up, based on the nature of requests, couple of times. Based on criticality, if I don’t hear after 3 follow ups, I will escalate either on my end or on your end.
    • Decision Making: I rely on evidence such as data and examples, and rational reasoning to make my decisions. If I am to be persuaded, I look for such support in particular - context, rationale and support/evidence – from you as well.  I clearly qualify when I feel strongly (and in which cases, I would have put thoughts into it, have supporting arguments), and when I feel neutral. If I am making a suggestion or a recommendation, I clearly qualify those as well, and I respect the boundaries of decision making and ownership.
  2. What I value:
    • Direct communication: I appreciate direct communication – both in terms of content and delivery
    • Any feedback any time: I welcome any feedback that you may want to provide about my work, my interaction, directly to me. A call/conversation is better. Feedback doesn’t necessarily need to wait till end of a project or scheduled formal feedback events.
    • Decision Making: I rely on evidence such as data and examples, and rational reasoning to make my decisions. If I am to be persuaded, I look for such support in particular - context, rationale and support/evidence – from you as well.  
  3. What I don’t have patience for

      4. I couldn’t fill this section with what I don’t have patience for. I am usually patient.

  4. What I don’t prefer or like:
    • Following up : I prefer not to follow up too many times.
    • Scheduling meetings
    • Someone not holding up their end of bargain (You say you are going to do something; I expect you to come through. Otherwise, you lose my respect/trust bit by bit). 
  5. How to best communicate with me
    • Instant messaging > Email > Calls for low priority/intensity communication. Sometimes calls > emails.
    • You can email or message/text me at any time. During work hours I will be online on Teams, which I occasionally check even during non-work hours on phone. (I recently turned off push notifications for Teams).
    • During workdays and hours, you can call me on my work phone, which redirects to my mobile phone. 
    • Front load your recommendations

  6. How to help me
    • I also appreciate if you can qualify your asks with a timing expectation from you.
    • IM > Emails > Calls for low intensity. But talk to me, instead of long drawn out emails
    • State/differentiate your opinions and positions clearly, and back it up with support
    • Listen to my opinions, positions, arguments and support
    • Schedule meetings if you can :)
  7. What people misunderstand about me
    • My work is not personal. (Not to say I won’t make friends at work).
    • My work disagreements are not personal.
    • When I am being a pain, I am mostly fulfilling my job duties – whether it is frequent follow ups or pushing for a certain decision.
    • My emails can come across terse, but I just believe in ‘to the point’ emails.
