Mafia

August 25, 2018
Games
One of my favorite party games and has so many fond memories attached to it. I first played this in State College's Kannada friends group - Sowmya, Manu, Deepika, Naveen, Ramya, Shekhar, Shruthi, Srinidhi, Shalini, Pradeep, Manasa, Sumanth. Mafia works great with a large group like this - and we played on almost all get togethers, 3-4 a year. I had never played a game like Mafia, and it was absolutely a revelation especially becuase it didn't need any material except lots of people and a good moderator. The role playing, bluffing, deceit - everything was so fun, and I didn't know any game that time which combined all these aspects - I mean I had played bluff in cards and there was kalla-police - those were the extent of my exposure to games. The game needs a good moderator and Srinidhi is a great one. I played the same game at Kolika's place during the MBA game nights and the rules were slightly different, and a different audience. The difference was interesting and how it allowed for a different experience and game play. Nevertheless had great fun. After MBA and SC friends moved on, I couldn't play as much. On the road trip to my wedding, together with couple of cousins we played this game on the bus. After few weeks, since I had introduced the game, I invented new ways to play - probably no one would have played like this - via Whatsapp ! I can't imagine getting people to play like that again sadly but it was great fun while it lasted. I changed so many rules, introduced my own rules, tweaked the game and learned so much. I wanted to write an analysis paper on my experiments - there was so much to say. Also only WhatsApp allowed us to play even though we were all far away, in different timezones, and allowed us to play for so long spanning days. It was great fun until someone started to question my rules and resisted to play according to the game spirit. Oh well, all good things come to end and it was getting a bit tiring after months of plays. Haven't had big groups to play after that and a card game I bought that promised to provide a shorter version and for a smaller group of players called 'One Night Mafia' is a big let down. Not only a letdown, it is flawed. Only aspect better about it is that it is accompanied by a mobile app, which acts as a moderator and relieves one person to play in the game. Earliest memory: 2009, State College Ease of learning: Easy Ease of playing: Easy My recommendation: 5/5
